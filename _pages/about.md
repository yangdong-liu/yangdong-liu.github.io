I am a first-year Ph.D. student at the **School of Journalism and Communication**, **Nanjing University**, under the supervision of Professor [Cheng-Jun Wang](https://chengjunwang.com/). I am also a member of the [Computational Communication Collaboratory](https://computational-communication.com/), Nanjing University. I completed my undergraduate studies at **South China University of Technology**, majoring in **Software Engineering**. My master's degree was pursued at the **China Foreign Affairs University**, majoring in **Political Science**.

My research interests include **computational communication**, **political communication** and **computational narrative**.pecifically, my study focuses on how **narrative and rhetoric**, as non-institutionalized factors, shape the behavioral preferences of political actors. My current research projects primarily involve two topics: 1) Whether some specific narratives and rhetoric, especially causal narratives, set behavioral preferences for political actors in US Congress and Chinese government. 2) Narrative strategies in public diplomacy.
